Hero demo

Paste Google coordinates. Get a weekly fungal-risk reading.

This public demo mirrors the DELTAPLANO bot logic: your pasted GPS point becomes the center of a circular footprint with a 50 meter radius, and DELTA Plant fuses a 7-day SAR moisture proxy with official Aeronautica Militare weather to produce a single weekly fungal-risk reading.

SAR means Synthetic Aperture Radar, an advanced sensing technology that sends electromagnetic signals to Earth and reads the return signal to produce high-resolution surface information even through clouds or darkness.

Scientific reading
Radar moisture signal

The SAR-style backscatter is used here as a wetness proxy: stronger surface moisture persistence usually raises the fungal-pressure baseline.

Weather support

Humidity and rainfall are read from the official Aeronautica Militare weather service, then fused with radar persistence because fungal development needs sustained wet conditions rather than an isolated rain event.

L-band interpretation

NISAR L-band is valuable because it keeps observing through clouds and gives useful sensitivity to near-surface moisture and vegetation structure.

How the demo works

The pasted Google coordinate defines the GPS center. DELTA Plant then inspects a circular area with a 50 m radius, equivalent to the same micro-footprint used in the bot workflow, and compresses the output into a single weekly mean risk.

Weather source

Temperature, humidity, and rainfall are pulled from the Servizio Meteorologico dell'Aeronautica Militare through the public Open-Meteo Archive endpoint exposed to the browser.
